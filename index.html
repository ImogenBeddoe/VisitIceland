VIDEOS

Renyisfara (Black Sand Beach) is located on the South Coast of Iceland, just beside the small fishing village of Vik I Myrdal. The beach is best known as Black Sand Beach. The black sand is created from heavily eroded volcanic rocks (artic adventures n.d.). Renyisfara is one of the top attractions to visit in Iceland due to its natural beauty due to its huge basalt stacks and powerful Atlantic waves. The beach was voted top 10 non-tropical beaches to visit by National Geographic in 1991 (Guide to Iceland n.d.).

Strokuur Geyser is one of Iceland’s most persistent geysers. Located in the Haukadalur valley (Geysir geothermal area), South-West Iceland. Strokkur is powerful hot spring which erupts between every 4-8 minutes and sprouts water to height around15-20 meters (Guide to Iceland n.d.).

Rangarping Eystra, located in the southern Icelandic Region. The area is best known for being the home to Skogafoss Waterfall. The Waterfall stands at 60 meters tall and with a width of 25 meters (icelandtravel n.d.). 572 steps lead you to the top of Skyogafoss which provides an scenic view.
